**Dataset Information:**
- **Name:** Bangladesh Air Quality Index (AQI) Dataset (2000–2025)
- **Version:** 2.0.0
- **DOI:** 10.17632/9j447cynb9.2
- **Published:** 21 January 2026
- **Contributor:** Tapon Paul, Daffodil International University, Dhaka, Bangladesh
- **Description:**  
  This dataset provides historical hourly air pollution measurements across **103 cities in Bangladesh**, spanning 25 years (2000–2025). It contains **1,048,551 hourly records**, making it one of the most comprehensive publicly available air quality datasets for Bangladesh. Coverage includes urban, divisional, district, sub-district, coastal, and border regions, offering extensive spatial and temporal breadth.

  Each record includes concentrations of eight key air pollutants — PM10, PM2.5, CO, CO2, NO2, SO2, and O3 — along with a composite Air Quality Index (AQI) value. Additional attributes include city identifiers, city names, geographic coordinates (latitude and longitude), and timestamps in ISO 8601 format.

  Temporal coverage varies by location. Earlier records are concentrated in major metropolitan areas such as Dhaka, Chittagong, and Sylhet, while more recent years include expanded coverage of smaller cities and upazilas. Geographic coverage spans all major regions of Bangladesh, including the Ganges–Brahmaputra–Meghna delta, coastal zones such as Cox's Bāzār and Teknāf, and northern border areas like Panchagarh and Thākurgaon.

  AQI values are calculated according to the **United States Environmental Protection Agency (US EPA)** standard and categorized into six levels:
  - Good
  - Moderate
  - Unhealthy for Sensitive Groups
  - Unhealthy
  - Very Unhealthy
  - Hazardous

  The dataset is provided in a standardized CSV format with 13 columns, allowing straightforward integration with statistical analysis, visualization, and machine learning pipelines.
